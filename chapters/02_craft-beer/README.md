# Book II · Craft Beer

> *"Where grain and water meet patience, beer is born."*

Welcome to **Craft Beer** — the second book of the *iGulu Home Brewing Bible*.

If *Book I · Foundations* taught you the universal grammar of fermentation, this book teaches the most extensively documented dialect — the world of beer. From the abbey-brewed ales of medieval Belgium to the West Coast IPAs of 1990s California, beer is humanity's longest continuous brewing tradition, and the one where home brewers most easily find a thriving global community.

This book brings that tradition into your kitchen.

---

## What This Chapter Covers

Book II is organized around four families of beer:

### 1. Ales — The Top-Fermented Tradition
The world of IPAs, Pale Ales, Wheat Beers, Belgian styles, Stouts, and Porters. Warmer fermentation, fruitier esters, faster turnaround. The most beginner-friendly entry point into beer brewing.

### 2. Lagers — The Cold and Patient Family
Pilsners, Helles, Märzen, Dunkel. Cold fermentation, clean flavors, long conditioning. Demanding precise temperature control — and rewarding patience with some of the cleanest, most refreshing beers ever made.

### 3. Sours — The Wild and the Funky
Gose, Berliner Weisse, Lambic styles. Beers fermented with lactobacillus, pediococcus, brettanomyces — embracing the wild microbes that mainstream brewing tries to exclude. Sours bridge naturally into *Book IV · The Living Culture*.

### 4. Specialty — Smoked, Fruited, Spiced, Barrel-Aged
The creative frontier of beer brewing. Where rules are negotiated with results, and where the most personal recipes emerge.

## How This Book Connects to iGulu

The iGulu system's precise temperature control and clean fermentation environment make it particularly well-suited to the lager and clean-ale families, where small temperature variations dramatically affect flavor. RFID recipe packs in this book will cover:

- Approachable starter ales (Pale Ale, Hefeweizen, American Wheat) — 2–3 week timelines
- BJCP-aligned classics (West Coast IPA, Munich Helles, Belgian Tripel) — 3–6 week timelines
- Sours and specialty experiments — 6+ week timelines

---

## How to Read This Book

We recommend:

1. Read *Book I · Foundations* first — especially the sections on water, yeast, and sanitation.
2. Start with an **American Wheat** or **Pale Ale** — short timelines, forgiving recipes.
3. Move to a **Belgian Witbier** or **Munich Helles** when you're comfortable with temperature control.
4. Try a **Sour** when you're ready to embrace unpredictability.

## Contributing to Book II

We welcome contributions that:

- Cover BJCP-recognized styles with tested, reproducible recipes
- Document regional or family beer traditions (e.g., a specific brewery's house style, or a regional folk recipe)
- Explore the sour and wild frontier with safety guidance
- Cover non-traditional or experimental brews with clear rationale

We are **less likely** to merge contributions that:

- Replicate already well-covered styles without new perspective
- Skip critical food-safety guidance for wild fermentation
- Promote specific commercial yeasts or ingredients without open-source alternatives
